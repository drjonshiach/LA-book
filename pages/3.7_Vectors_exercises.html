Exercise 3.1

The points \(U\), \(V\) and \(W\) have the following position vectors:

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \mathbf{u} &= \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}, & \mathbf{v} &= \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}, & \mathbf{w} &= \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix}. \end{align*} \end{split}\]

Find:

â€ƒ (a) â€ƒ \(2 \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{w}\)

â€ƒ (b) â€ƒ \(\mathbf{w} - \mathbf{u}\)

â€ƒ (c) â€ƒ a unit vector pointing in the same direction of \(\mathbf{u}\)

â€ƒ (d) â€ƒ a unit vector pointing in the opposite direction of \(\mathbf{v}\)

â€ƒ (e) â€ƒ a vector pointing in the same direction as \(\mathbf{v}\)

â€ƒ (f) â€ƒ \(\overrightarrow{UV}\)

â€ƒ (g) â€ƒ \(\overrightarrow{UW}\)

â€ƒ (h) â€ƒ \(\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{w}\)

â€ƒ (i) â€ƒ the angle \(\angle VUW\)

â€ƒ (j) â€ƒ show that \(\mathbf{u}\) is at right angles to \(\mathbf{v}\)

â€ƒ (k) â€ƒ \(\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{w}\)

Solution to Exercise 3.1

â€ƒ (a) â€ƒ \(2\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{w} = 2\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 12 \end{pmatrix}\);

â€ƒ (b) â€ƒ \(\mathbf{w} - \mathbf{u} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 - 2 \\ 6 - 3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}\);

â€ƒ (c) â€ƒ \(\hat{\mathbf{u}} = \dfrac{\mathbf{u}}{|\mathbf{u}|} = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{13}} \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} \frac{2}{\sqrt{13}} \\ \frac{3}{\sqrt{13}} \end{pmatrix}\);

â€ƒ (d) â€ƒ \(-\hat{\mathbf{v}} = -\dfrac{\mathbf{v}}{|\mathbf{v}|} = -\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{13}} \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -\frac{3}{\sqrt{13}} \\ \frac{2}{\sqrt{13}} \end{pmatrix}\);

â€ƒ (e) â€ƒ \(\dfrac{1}{2}\mathbf{v} = \dfrac{1}{2} \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 / 2 \\ -2 / 2 \end{pmatrix}\);

â€ƒ (f) â€ƒ \(\overrightarrow{UV} = \mathbf{v} - \mathbf{u} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}= \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -5 \end{pmatrix}\);

â€ƒ (g) â€ƒ \(\overrightarrow{UW} = \mathbf{w} - \mathbf{u} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}\);

â€ƒ (h) â€ƒ \(\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{w} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} \cdot \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix} = 2 \times 1 + 3 \times 6 = 20\);

â€ƒ (i) â€ƒ Using equation (3.4)

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \overrightarrow{UV} \cdot \overrightarrow{UW} &= |\overrightarrow{UV}||\overrightarrow{UW}| \cos(\theta) \\ \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -5 \end{pmatrix} \cdot \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} &= \left| \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -5 \end{pmatrix} \right| \left| \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} \right| \cos(\theta) \\ -16 &= \sqrt{26} \sqrt{10} \cos(\theta) \\ \theta &= \cos^{-1} \left( \frac{-16}{\sqrt{260}} \right) \approx 3.0172 \end{align*} \end{split}\]

â€ƒ (j) â€ƒ \(\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} \cdot \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} = 2 \times 3 + 3 \times (-2) = 0\);

â€ƒ (k) â€ƒ \(\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{w} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ 3 & -2 & 0 \\ 1 & 6 & 0 \end{vmatrix} = 0\mathbf{i} - 0 \mathbf{j} + 20 \mathbf{k} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 20 \end{pmatrix}\).

Exercise 3.2

Write \(\mathbf{u} = (2,7,1)\) as:

â€ƒ (a) â€ƒ a linear combination of \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\), \(\begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).

â€ƒ (b) â€ƒ a linear combination of vectors \(\mathbf{f}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}, \mathbf{f}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{f}_3 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}\).

Solution to Exercise 3.2

â€ƒ (a) â€ƒ \(\mathbf{u} = 2 \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} + 7 \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = 2 \mathbf{i} + 7 \mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}\);

â€ƒ (b)

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} & \left( \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -1 & 2 & 0 & 7 \\ 0 & 0 & -1 & 1 \end{array} \right) \begin{array}{l} \\ R_2 + R_1 \\ \phantom{x} \end{array} \\ \\ \longrightarrow \quad & \left( \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 2 & 1 & 9 \\ 0 & 0 & -1 & 1 \end{array} \right) \begin{array}{l} \\ \\ R_3 + R_1 \end{array} \\ \\ \longrightarrow \quad & \left( \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 1/2 & 9/2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -1 \end{array} \right) \begin{array}{l} R_1 - R_3 \\ R_1 - \frac{1}{2}R_3 \\ \phantom{x} \end{array} \\ \\ \longrightarrow \quad & \left( \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -1 \end{array} \right) \end{align*} \end{split}\]

Therefore \(\mathbf{u} = 3 \mathbf{f}_1 + 5 \mathbf{f}_2 - \mathbf{f}_3\).

Exercise 3.3

Find \(k\) such that the vectors \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\) are perpendicular:

â€ƒ (a) â€ƒ \(\mathbf{u} = (1, k, -2)\) and \(\mathbf{v} = (2, -5, 4)\) in \(\mathbb{R}^3\).

â€ƒ (b) â€ƒ \(\mathbf{u} =(1, 0, k+2, -1, 2)\) and \(\mathbf{v} = (1, k, -2, 1, 2)\) in \(\mathbb{R}^5\).

Solution to Exercise 3.3

â€ƒ (a) â€ƒ If \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\) are perpendicular then \(\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0\).

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} &= 0 \\ (1, k, -2) \cdot (2, -5, 4) &= 0 \\ 2 - 5 k - 8 &= 0 \\ -5k &= 6 \\ \therefore k &= -\frac{6}{5}. \end{align*} \end{split}\]

â€ƒ (b) â€ƒ

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} &= 0 \\ (1, 0, k+2, -1, 2) \cdot (1, k, -2, 1, 2) &= 0 \\ 1 - 2k - 2 - 1 + 2 &= 0 \\ -2k &= 0 \\ \therefore k &= 0. \end{align*} \end{split}\]

Exercise 3.4

Which pair of the following vectors is perpendicular? For the remaining pairs, what is the angle between them?

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \mathbf{u} &= \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}, & \mathbf{v} &= \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}, & \mathbf{w} &= \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}. \end{align*} \end{split}\]

Solution to Exercise 3.4

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} &= \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} \cdot \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix} = -1 + 4 - 3 = 0, \\ \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{w} &= \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} \cdot \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = 2 - 6 + 3 = -1, \\ \mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{w} &= \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix} \cdot \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = -2 -6 -1 = -9. \end{align*} \end{split}\]

Therefore \(\mathbf{u} \perp \mathbf{v}\). The angle between \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\) is

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \theta &= \cos^{-1} \left( \frac{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{w}}{|\mathbf{u} ||\mathbf{w}|} \right) \\ &= \cos^{-1} \left( \frac{-1}{\sqrt{14}\sqrt{14}}\right) \\ &= \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{-1}{14}\right) \approx 1.6423, \end{align*} \end{split}\]

and the angle between \(\mathbf{v}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\) is

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \theta &= \cos^{-1} \left( \frac{\mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{w}}{|\mathbf{v} ||\mathbf{w}|} \right) \\ &= \cos^{-1} \left( \frac{-9}{\sqrt{6}\sqrt{14}} \right) \approx 2.9515. \end{align*} \end{split}\]
