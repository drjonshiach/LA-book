Exercise 2.1

Solve the following linear system of equations using the inverse of the coefficient matrix.

(a) â€ƒ \( \begin{align*} - 4 x_{1} + 2 x_{2} &= -22, \\ 3 x_{1} + 4 x_{2} &= 11. \end{align*} \)

(b) â€ƒ \( \begin{align*} - 4 x_{1} + 2 x_{2} &= 6, \\ - x_{1} - 3 x_{2} &= -2. \end{align*} \)

â€ƒ

(c) â€ƒ \( \begin{align*} - 4 x_{1} - 4 x_{2} - 2 x_{3} &= 16, \\ 3 x_{1} + 4 x_{3} &= -8, \\ x_{1} &= 0. \end{align*} \)

(d) â€ƒ \( \begin{align*} 4 x_{1} - 4 x_{3} &= 8, \\ 4 x_{1} - x_{2} + x_{3} &= -4, \\ 3 x_{1} + x_{2} + 2 x_{3} &= -12. \end{align*} \)

Solution to Exercise 2.1

(a)

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \det \begin{pmatrix} -4 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \\ \end{pmatrix} &= -22, \\ \operatorname{adj} \begin{pmatrix} -4 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \\ \end{pmatrix} &= \begin{pmatrix} 4 & -2 \\ -3 & -4 \\ \end{pmatrix}, \\ \therefore A^{-1} &= \frac{1}{-22} \begin{pmatrix} 4 & -2 \\ -3 & -4 \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} - 2/11 & 1/11 \\ 3/22 & 2/11 \\ \end{pmatrix} ,\end{align*} \end{split}\]

therefore

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \mathbf{{x}} = \begin{pmatrix} - 2/11 & 1/11 \\ 3/22 & 2/11 \\ \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} -22 \\ 11 \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ -1 \\ \end{pmatrix} \end{align*} \end{split}\]

(b)

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \det \begin{pmatrix} -4 & 2 \\ -1 & -3 \\ \end{pmatrix} &= 14, \\ \operatorname{adj} \begin{pmatrix} -4 & 2 \\ -1 & -3 \\ \end{pmatrix} &= \begin{pmatrix} -3 & -2 \\ 1 & -4 \\ \end{pmatrix}, \\ \therefore A^{-1} &= \frac{1}{14} \begin{pmatrix} -3 & -2 \\ 1 & -4 \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -3/14 & -1/7 \\ 1/14 & -2/7 \\ \end{pmatrix}, \end{align*} \end{split}\]

therefore

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \mathbf{{x}} = \begin{pmatrix} -3/14 & -1/7 \\ 1/14 & -2/7 \\ \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ -2 \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \\ \end{pmatrix} \end{align*} \end{split}\]

(c)

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \det \begin{pmatrix} -4 & -4 & -2 \\ 3 & 0 & 4 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ \end{pmatrix} &= -16, \\ \operatorname{adj} \begin{pmatrix} -4 & -4 & -2 \\ 3 & 0 & 4 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ \end{pmatrix} &= \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & -16 \\ 4 & 2 & 10 \\ 0 & -4 & 12 \\ \end{pmatrix}, \\ \therefore A^{-1} &= \frac{1}{-16} \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & -16 \\ 4 & 2 & 10 \\ 0 & -4 & 12 \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \\ - 1/4 & -1/8 & -5/8 \\ 0 & 1/4 & -3/4 \\ \end{pmatrix}, \end{align*} \end{split}\]

therefore

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \mathbf{{x}} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \\ - 1/4 & -1/8 & -5/8 \\ 0 & 1/4 & -3/4 \\ \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 16 \\ -8 \\ 0 \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ -3 \\ -2 \\ \end{pmatrix} \end{align*} \end{split}\]

(d)

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \det \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 0 & -4 \\ 4 & -1 & 1 \\ 3 & 1 & 2 \\ \end{pmatrix} &= -40, \\ \operatorname{adj} \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 0 & -4 \\ 4 & -1 & 1 \\ 3 & 1 & 2 \\ \end{pmatrix} &= \begin{pmatrix} -3 & -4 & -4 \\ -5 & 20 & -20 \\ 7 & -4 & -4 \\ \end{pmatrix}, \\ \therefore A^{-1} &= \frac{1}{-40} \begin{pmatrix} -3 & -4 & -4 \\ -5 & 20 & -20 \\ 7 & -4 & -4 \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3/40 & 1/10 & 1/10 \\ 1/8 & -1/2 & 1/2 \\ -7/40 & 1/10 & 1/10 \\ \end{pmatrix},\end{align*} \end{split}\]

therefore

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \mathbf{{x}} = \begin{pmatrix} 3/40 & 1/10 & 1/10 \\ 1/8 & -1/2 & 1/2 \\ -7/40 & 1/10 & 1/10 \\ \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 8 \\ -4 \\ -12 \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ -3 \\ -3 \\ \end{pmatrix} \end{align*} \end{split}\]

Solution to Exercise 2.2

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} x_{1} &= \frac{\det \left(\begin{matrix}-20 & 4\\-5 & 1\end{matrix}\right)}{\det \left(\begin{matrix}1 & 4\\-4 & 1\end{matrix}\right)} = \frac{0}{17} = 0, \\ \\ x_{2} &= \frac{\det \left(\begin{matrix}1 & -20\\-4 & -5\end{matrix}\right)}{\det \left(\begin{matrix}1 & 4\\-4 & 1\end{matrix}\right)} = \frac{-85}{17} = -5, \\ \\ \end{align*} \end{split}\]

(b)

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} x_{1} &= \frac{\det \left(\begin{matrix}4 & 1\\12 & 4\end{matrix}\right)}{\det \left(\begin{matrix}1 & 1\\0 & 4\end{matrix}\right)} = \frac{4}{4} = 1, \\ \\ x_{2} &= \frac{\det \left(\begin{matrix}1 & 4\\0 & 12\end{matrix}\right)}{\det \left(\begin{matrix}1 & 1\\0 & 4\end{matrix}\right)} = \frac{12}{4} = 3, \\ \\ \end{align*} \end{split}\]

(c)

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} x_{1} &= \frac{\det \left(\begin{matrix}21 & -4 & -4\\8 & -1 & -1\\-14 & -1 & 3\end{matrix}\right)}{\det \left(\begin{matrix}3 & -4 & -4\\-2 & -1 & -1\\4 & -1 & 3\end{matrix}\right)} = \frac{44}{-44} = -1, \\ \\ x_{2} &= \frac{\det \left(\begin{matrix}3 & 21 & -4\\-2 & 8 & -1\\4 & -14 & 3\end{matrix}\right)}{\det \left(\begin{matrix}3 & -4 & -4\\-2 & -1 & -1\\4 & -1 & 3\end{matrix}\right)} = \frac{88}{-44} = -2, \\ \\ x_{3} &= \frac{\det \left(\begin{matrix}3 & -4 & 21\\-2 & -1 & 8\\4 & -1 & -14\end{matrix}\right)}{\det \left(\begin{matrix}3 & -4 & -4\\-2 & -1 & -1\\4 & -1 & 3\end{matrix}\right)} = \frac{176}{-44} = -4, \\ \\ \end{align*} \end{split}\]

(d)

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} x_{1} &= \frac{\det \left(\begin{matrix}5 & 4 & 1\\-1 & 1 & 1\\-14 & -4 & 2\end{matrix}\right)}{\det \left(\begin{matrix}4 & 4 & 1\\-2 & 1 & 1\\-5 & -4 & 2\end{matrix}\right)} = \frac{0}{33} = 0, \\ \\ x_{2} &= \frac{\det \left(\begin{matrix}4 & 5 & 1\\-2 & -1 & 1\\-5 & -14 & 2\end{matrix}\right)}{\det \left(\begin{matrix}4 & 4 & 1\\-2 & 1 & 1\\-5 & -4 & 2\end{matrix}\right)} = \frac{66}{33} = 2, \\ \\ x_{3} &= \frac{\det \left(\begin{matrix}4 & 4 & 5\\-2 & 1 & -1\\-5 & -4 & -14\end{matrix}\right)}{\det \left(\begin{matrix}4 & 4 & 1\\-2 & 1 & 1\\-5 & -4 & 2\end{matrix}\right)} = \frac{-99}{33} = -3, \\ \\ \end{align*} \end{split}\]

Solution to Exercise 2.9

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} & \left( \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 3 & 1 \\ 4 & 3 & k & 2 \\ 2 & 1 & 2 & 3 \end{array} \right) \begin{array}{l} \phantom{x} \\ R_2 - 4 R_1 \\ R_3 - 2 R_1 \end{array} \\ \\ \longrightarrow \quad & \left( \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 3 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 & k - 12 & -2 \\ 0 & -1 & -4 & 1 \end{array} \right) \begin{array}{l} \phantom{x} \\ R_2 \leftrightarrow R_3 \\ \phantom{x} \end{array} \\ \\ \longrightarrow \quad & \left( \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 3 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 & -4 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 & k - 12 & -2 \end{array} \right) \begin{array}{l} \phantom{x} \\ \phantom{x} \\ R_3 - R_2 \end{array} \\ \\ \longrightarrow \quad & \left( \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 3 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 & -4 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & k - 8 & -3 \end{array} \right). \end{align*} \end{split}\]

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 4 & 3 & k \\ 2 & 1 & 2 \end{vmatrix} = \begin{vmatrix} 3 & k \\ 1 & 2 \end{vmatrix} - \begin{vmatrix} 4 & k \\ 2 & 2 \end{vmatrix} + 3 \begin{vmatrix} 4 & 3 \\ 2 & 1 \end{vmatrix} = 6 - k - 8 + 2k - 6 = k - 8. \end{align*} \end{split}\]

For the system to have a solution the determinant must be non-zero, therefore this system has a solution when \(k \neq 8\).
