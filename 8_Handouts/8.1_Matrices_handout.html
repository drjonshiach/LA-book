  • Matrix addition/subtraction: \([A \pm B]_{ij} = a_{ij} \pm b_{ij}\)

Example 1.3

Evaluate the following:

â€ƒ (i) â€ƒ \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 \end{pmatrix}\) â€ƒ (ii) â€ƒ \(\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 7 \\ -11 \\ -13 \end{pmatrix}\) â€ƒ (iii) â€ƒ \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 3 & 5 \\ 7 & 9 & 11 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix}2 & 3 \\ 5 & 7 \end{pmatrix}\)

Example 1.4

â€ƒ (i) â€ƒ \(2 \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{pmatrix}\) â€ƒ (ii) â€ƒ \(\dfrac{1}{2} \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 3 & 2 \\ 4 & -2 \end{pmatrix}\) â€ƒ (iii) â€ƒ \(\dfrac{1}{3} \begin{pmatrix}1 & 6 & 4 \\ 0 & 3 & -1 \end{pmatrix}\) â€ƒ (iv) â€ƒ \(101 \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} - 99 \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\)

  • Matrix multiplication: \([AB]_{ij} = \displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^p a_{ik}b_{kj}\) where \(p\) is the number of columns in \(A\) and the number of rows in \(B\)

â€ƒ (i) â€ƒ \(A^2\) â€ƒ (ii) â€ƒ \(A^3\) â€ƒ (iii) â€ƒ \(A^5\)

  • Transpose of a matrix: \([A^\mathrm{T}]_{ij}=a_{ji}\)

  • Identity matrix: \([I]_{ij} = \begin{cases} 1 & i = j, \\ 0 & \text{elsewhere} \end{cases}\)

  • Symmetric matrix: \([A]_{ij} = [A]_{ji}\)

Example 1.9

â€ƒ (i) â€ƒ \(A=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 2 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\) â€ƒ (ii) â€ƒ \(B=\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 & -3 \\ 0 & 5 & 7 \\ -3 & 7 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\) â€ƒ (iii) â€ƒ \(C=\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 3 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\)

  • Determinant of a \(2\times 2\) matrix: \(\det\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} = \begin{vmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{vmatrix} = ad - bc\)

Example 1.10

â€ƒ (i) â€ƒ \(\begin{vmatrix} 5 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{vmatrix}\) â€ƒ (ii) â€ƒ \(\det \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ ka & kb \end{pmatrix}\) â€ƒ (iii) â€ƒ \(\begin{vmatrix} 5 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 \end{vmatrix}\)

  • Minor: \(M_{ij} = \) the determinant of the matrix formed by omitting row \(i\) and column \(j\)

Example 1.11

Given the matrix \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 & 9 \end{pmatrix},\) calculate:

â€ƒ (i) â€ƒ \(M_{11}\) â€ƒ (ii) â€ƒ \(M_{12}\) â€ƒ (iii) â€ƒ \(M_{13}\)

  • Cofactor: \(C_{ij} = (-1)^{i+j}M_{ij}\)

  • Determinant of an \(n\times n\) matrix: \(\det(A) = \displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^n a_{ik} C_{ik} = \displaystyle\sum_{j=1}^n a_{kj} C_{kj}\)

Example 1.13

\[\begin{split}\begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 & 4 & 3 \\ 2 & 0 & 5 & -3 \\ 1 & 2 & 4 & 5 \\ 2 & 0 & -2 & 4 \end{pmatrix}.\end{split}\]

  • Inverse matrix: \(A^{-1}\) such that \(A^{-1}A = AA^{-1} = I\)

Example 1.14

Given the two matrices \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(B = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix},\) show that \(B\) is the inverse of \(A\).

  • Adjoint of a matrix: \([\operatorname{adj}(A)]_{ij} = (-1)^{i+j}M_{ij}\)

â€ƒ (i) â€ƒ \(\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}\) â€ƒ (ii) â€ƒ \(\begin{pmatrix} 5 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{pmatrix}\) â€ƒ (iii) â€ƒ \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 3 \\ 4 & -2 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}\)

  • Adjoint-determinant formula: \(A^{-1} = \dfrac{\operatorname{adj}(A)}{\det(A)}\)
